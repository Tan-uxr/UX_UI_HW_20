    Watchify

    Tv and movie browsing experience curated unique to the user's lifestyle and preferences

    • Role: UX Researcher & Analysis
    • Responsibilities: User Interviews & Insights, Guerrilla Testing, Competitor Analysis, User Persona, User Journey Map, Features, and Mid-High fidelity.
    • Team Size: 5 members
    • Duration: Two weeks

    Project Overview

    • Problem: TV and movie watchers are overwhelmed by the abundance of content on streaming platforms and suspicious of generic recommendations which do not reflect their personal tastes and moods.
    • Solution: User Interviews & Insights, Create a mobile app wich provides a tv and movie browsing experience curated unique to the user’s lifestyle and preferences.
    • Results: Users were satified because the app facilitate their search based on connect highly personal watch-style with the people close to the user which make easier to find content.

    Design Process

    Research: Quantitavie Insights (30 participants online survey) We identify that users don’t want to spend lots of time checking different websites to find something to watch and then figure out what services the movie is available on. From an external resource we found that in 2020 Netflix users spent an average of 17.8 minutes per day = 84 hours annually. Qualitative Insights (five User interviews) We noticed that users don’t trust streaming services recommendations (people have eclectic tastes) Users get recommendations from various sources,( including social media sites/apps, news articles, reviews, streaming platforms, lists of award winners.) When people are searching for new things to watch, we identified 2 points: a)They mainly trust in friends’ and family (because it is a way to socialize and build common interests) b)Their choice depends on their mood based on how their day was.

    Definition & Synthesis: After concluding the research phase, we moved on to defining our problem statement, which we revised several times Our final version: TV and movie watchers are overwhelmed by the abundance of content on streaming platforms and are suspicious of generic recommendations which do not reflect their personal tastes and moods. We then conducted a competitor analysis. Unsurprisingly, we found that this is quite a crowded marketplace: direct competitors like Likewise, Letterboxd and Stamp offer recommendations for things to watch as well as the ability to interact with a community of fellow users; indirect competitors include Netflix and other streaming platforms, as well as Youtube and even Spotify. However, we found that our direct competitors’ apps are difficult to navigate and lack the ability to easily create watchlists and share them with a close circle of friends and family. We then created a storyboard to help us understand how users might experience and interact with our app: the scenario is someone getting ready for a second date; he hears about Watchify from a friend and finds a watchlist which his date has created; this removes the anxiety about finding the perfect movie for their date.

    Iteration: We then moved onto the ideation phase, using the ‘I Like, I Wish, What If’ method. We voted on our favorite ideas and created a shortlist of around 6 features (2 from each category) which you see here. We mapped out these ideas on a Feature Prioritization Matrix and found that the most important features for us to prioritize were the ability to create a personal watchlist, to share that watchlist with friends, and to search for shows based on mood Focusing on these three features, we were able to create our user flow and then start working on our paper wireframes.

    Prototype: So, we all individually created paper prototypes, and thankfully, due to the amount of collaborative work and discussions beforehand, our prototypes all contained similar features. Nonetheless it was amazing seeing how everyone on the team visualized them. We walked through each together and decided on one to test with users which had most of the features we all wanted. The feedback we got back from these tests were incredibly helpful and we have highlighted some of the major ones we received and made notes on for our next iteration. Even though this version was very low fidelity, it was nice to hear from our tests that users would be interested in an app such as this. Once we made a list of all the changes to create, we all set off to create different frames of the app individually. We agreed beforehand on some style guidelines and worked together in Figma in order to create the best we could in the time allotted. Once every feature was created and set up, we began our next round of testing. It was extremely gratifying having potential users of the app see a prototype of our application that was much closer to what we all envisioned. The feedback we received made all of us on the team realize the true potential of what our final product could be.

    User testing: We took all of our research and insights and put together a clickable prototype. Our feature prioritization process led us to three main features of the app. We want our users to be able to find movies and shows to watch based on their mood, to create and share watchlists with friends and family, and to follow friends and see the watchlists they’ve created. All three of these features are aimed towards helping our users find trustworthy and interesting things to watch. When it came to user testing of our prototypes, the three tasks we asked our user testers to work through were as follows: Find a movie that makes you happy, Create and share a watchlist, and Search for a friend and view one of their watchlists. Many of our testers mentioned that they’d like the social and sharing aspect of our app to be a little clearer and more prominent.

    onclusion & Future Opportunities: Many of our testers mentioned that they’d like the social and sharing aspect of our app to be a little clearer and more prominent. Once we’ve edited the mid-fi prototype to accommodate the most important changes suggested by our testers, we plan to create a high-fidelity clickable prototype for a third round of user testing.The hi-fi prototype will resolve any visual design inconsistencies across each of the screens. In order to bring the personal and relatable functions of the app more into focus, we will bring watchlists created by friends and recommended by people with similar interests to the forefront. The five of us worked quite well together and enjoyed the research process we felt like we addressed the very common problem of having a trusted resource to rely on for film and TV recommendations. Our conversations with the people we interviewed and the testers confirmed our hypothesis that the movie-finding process can be overwhelming and impersonal. We hope our app has proposed a viable solution to this problem. Since everyone’s connections to what they watch are highly personal, and since the people close to us know us the best, we want to make it easy and straightforward to find content that each person who uses our app will enjoy.

    PGK

    Re-design a website for a Non-Profit Organization

    • Role: UX Researcher & Analysis
    • Responsibilities: Stakeholder Interview and insights, Problem Statement, heuristic Evaluation, Proto Persona & User Persona, Competitor Analysis, User Journey Map, Moodboard, Prototyping & User testing.
    • Team Size: 5 members
    • Duration: Two weeks

    Project Overview

    • Problem: Parents with young and adolescent children struggle to find volunteering opportunities because the activities are not framed in a way that is engaging for children.
    • Solution: We believe that creating a website that appeals to children and encourages them to participate in their community will attract more visitors and increase user engagement.
    • Results: Users were satified because the website redesign was more intuitive and appealing.

    Design Process

    Research: We had two interviews. The first was with Cristin O'Leary Jones the Executive Director and the second with Molly Yuska the Founder of PGK. Through these conversations, we learned more about their brand identity, experience and what they need from their website. The purpose of PGK is to introduce people to community service, offer entry points to help them get involved, and to understand how meaningful it is. What makes this volunteer organization unique is that it offers projects for young children 13 and under as well as kids 14 and over. The current website was created exclusively for children 13 and under with a parent orientated design with the central assumption that parents are the only ones who make decisions about how and where their children will engage in community service. Our user interviews gave us the opposite point of view that children need to be more involved to be engaged. The website also has two other important audiences: teachers who can find ways of integrating community services with specific lessons for different school levels and other non profit organizations who want to participate as partners of PGK. The goal is to give children access to a variety of educational materials and resources to reinforce their learning about specific causes with the support of parents, teachers, and organizations.The stakeholders were very enthusiastic about our redesign proposal because they recognize that the website has an old design and they want one that will be the easiest possible for parents and kids. Also, they consider that this website should be more about the why behind community service, the benefits of it, and stories about volunteer work. The potential user of this website is a busy mother who works as a teacher and wants to enhance her child’s compassion and empathy. She believes that community service presents a great opportunity to teach and learn these important values. However she doesn’t have enough time to find a social network and she is also concerned about the legitimacy of the volunteer activities from an online search.Based on user interviews, we have identified parents' motivations and goals when they try to get their children involved in volunteer service. For example: 1. They want to raise emotionally stable children with the character and ethics that are learned through compassion and gratitude. 2. They want to invest in their communities to gain a sense of belonging and social responsibility. Children are organically centered on their own lives. They are more focused on friends, sports, and games than they are on the sufferings and misfortunes of others. Helping children engage in community service can be a significant challenge for parents. One of the core strategies is to “allow kids to choose volunteer activities or they will be bored.” The website must be designed for children as the core users. Our key takeaways or opportunities are: a) Design a child user friendly website where kids feel excited to be engaged and learn.b) Make the website more visual and gamified. c) Display information in a child friendly, fun and meaningful way.
